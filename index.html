Scope and Mission

The IEEE CIS Task Force on Physics-informed AI, Evolutionary Learning and Optimization is dedicated to advancing computational intelligence for physical, engineering and scientific systems by integrating physical laws, mathematical models, data-driven learning and optimisation. The task force will promote Physics-informed AI as a broader agenda that includes physics-informed neural networks, neural operators, differentiable simulation, probabilistic scientific machine learning, trustworthy AI, evolutionary computation and decision-oriented optimisation. Its mission is to develop learning, inference, optimisation and control methods that can operate under multi-scale dynamics, sparse and noisy data, irregular geometries, uncertain boundary conditions and coupled multi-physics constraints.

The IEEE Task Force on PiELaO aims to enhance the cutting-edge computational intelligence methods by leveraging the strengths of data-driven AI approaches and physical models, and bring together academic researchers and business practitioners to achieve both theoretical breakthroughs and industrial innovation. The key objectives are: first, to consolidate Physics-informed AI as a computational intelligence agenda that connects learning, optimisation, uncertainty quantification and physical modelling; second, to retain evolutionary computation as a distinctive methodological pillar for solving optimisation, inverse-problem and experimental-design challenges in physical systems; third, to build an interdisciplinary community across academia, industry and public research organisations; and fourth, to develop seminars, workshops, special issues, benchmarks and open resources that support reproducible and deployable Physics-informed AI.

The task force will organise its technical activities through six coordinated tracks.

Track 1: Mathematical and physical foundations of Physics-informed AI. This track will examine how conservation laws, governing equations, boundary conditions, interface conditions, symmetries and dynamical constraints can be embedded into learning systems. It will cover identifiability, stability, convergence, function-space learning, irregular domains and multi-scale physical systems.

Track 2: Physics-informed learning architectures. This track will advance model classes for scientific and engineering systems, including PINNs, neural operators, graph neural operators, implicit neural representations, differentiable simulators, hybrid physics-data models and surrogate models for forward, inverse and control problems.

Track 3: Physics-guided optimisation and evolutionary computation. This track will retain the distinctive PiELaO contribution by developing evolutionary algorithms, multi-objective optimisation, adaptive collocation-point selection, loss balancing, optimal experimental design, meta-learning, transfer optimisation and evolutionary multitasking for physics-informed learning.

Track 4: Data-scarce, uncertain and trustworthy scientific AI. This track will address sparse observations, expensive labels, noisy measurements, incomplete physics and distribution shift. It will cover Bayesian inference, uncertainty quantification, posterior sampling, conformal prediction, selective prediction, out-of-distribution detection, synthetic data generation and validation protocols.

Track 5: Scalable and deployable Physics-informed AI systems. This track will focus on GPU acceleration, high-performance computing, distributed training, real-time inference, edge deployment, reproducible software, open-source libraries, benchmarks and standardised evaluation workflows.

Track 6: Multi-physics applications and industrial translation. This track will connect methodological advances to application domains such as electromagnetics, heat transfer, fluid-structure interaction, quantum systems, biomedical engineering, geophysical monitoring, environmental modelling, manufacturing, non-destructive evaluation and digital twins. Each application will be used to identify methodological challenges such as coupled PDEs, sparse sensing, irregular geometry, multi-scale behaviour and safety-critical decision-making.

Planned Regional Activities

The primary goal of the PiELaO task force is to promote the newly advanced physics-informed evolutionary learning and optimization research and build a linkage between the PiELaO researchers and practitioners to achieve research and technological innovation across the Asian and British Regions. We have successfully organized several research activities (below) in the past years and identified the PiELaO critical mass in the evolutionary computation community.

  • Special Session on Physics-informed Computational Intelligence: Theories, Models and Applications in the 2022 IEEE Symposium Series on Computational Intelligence
  • Special Session on Physics-Informed Computational Intelligence: Algorithms and Applications in the 2024 IEEE World Congress on Computational Intelligence
  • Workshop on Physics-Informed Evolutionary Learning and Optimization in the 2025 IEEE Congress on Evolutionary Computation

Accordingly, we are planning a series of academic workshops, journal special issues and academic and enterprise engagement events over the next two years.

Year 1: We are organizing one special issue on Physics-Informed Evolutionary Computation: Advances and Applications in IEEE Transactions on Evolutionary Computation in 2025 (the proposal has been accepted). In additional, we will collaborate with industrial partners, national/regional funding agency and research institutes to organize academic and enterprise engagement events and grow a wider PiELaO community. One potential event is to be organized in the Summer/Winter of 2025.

Year 2: We will submit one special issue to IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems, and organize one workshop in either IEEE World Congress on Computational Intelligence, IEEE Symposium Series on Computational Intelligence, or IEEE CIS-sponsored ones. Another academic and enterprise engagement event will be organized in 2026.

We will organize a regular online technical seminar joined by invited academic/industrial speakers on PiELaO and plan the activities in a quarterly task force committee meeting.

In addition, we aim to provide sustained support and foster long-term growth within the PiELaO community. While special sessions and issues offer valuable platforms for sharing research, we will go beyond these efforts by developing collaborative networks across different regions including Aisa, Europe and USA, developing benchmarks for some specific application domains and offering open resources of PiELaO in the implementations. More importantly, we will strengthen the foundation of the PiELaO community by joining forces with key stakeholders and leveraging diverse expertise over the regions. We will exploit multiple communication channels (e.g., newsletters, social media, webinars, conferences) to reach different segments of the PiELaO community and partnerships with other organizations to extend the PiELaO impact.
